11 charged with violent disorder after mass fight breaks out at Luton Airport

Publish date: 2024-09-11

A Wild West-style mass brawl erupted between passengers at London Luton Airport as video emerged of the group fighting and throwing luggages at the departure lounge.

At least three people were taken to hospital with serious injuries and 17 people were arrested, police confirmed the incident which took place at around 8am on Friday, May 14.

Viral video taken by a fellow passenger at the departure lounge shows a group of men chasing another group with their carry-on luggages.

Some people can be heard screaming "stop" when the group started punching and kicking each other on the benches in the waiting area.

At least two men are thrown to the floor and knocked unconscious.

A woman working at a nearby duty-shop is seen closing the doors as the group brawl in front of the shop.

Terrified bystanders are seen calling for help and moving further away from the fight before airport security arrives.

The guards run after the men and manage to pin down some members near the escalators at the centre of the lounge.

Pictures shared online show blood splattered across the floor in front of duty-free shops.

Bedfordshire Police said 17 people were arrested and it was investigating the "violent disorder", adding: "Police were made aware of a violent disorder at London Luton Airport.

"Four people were injured, and three were taken to hospital with serious injuries.

"Subsequently, 17 people were arrested and remain in police custody for questioning."

A spokesperson for London Luton Airport told Daily Star: "We are shocked and saddened by this isolated incident.

"We take a zero tolerance approach to violence and continue to assist the police with their enquiries.

"We would like to apologise to any passengers affected."

Later on Saturday, Bedfordshire Police confirmed 11 of the arrested men have been charged with violent disorder and have been released on bail until a future court hearing.

Out of the remaining people arrested; five were released with no further action and one was released on bail with conditions.
