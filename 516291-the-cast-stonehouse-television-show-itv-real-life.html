Who is Stonehouse based on?

ITV's drama series Stonehouse is based on the real life of John Stonehouse. He was a Labour MP (1957-1976) for Wednesbury and later Walsall North. John was a charismatic and quite young figure in the famous party, which helped him gather early momentum in his political career.

He secured several positions, including Postmaster General and Minister of State for Technology. However, all that promise died when John Stonehouse became caught in a bizarre scandal. He tried to fake his death and moved to Australia to start a new life.

Did John Stonehouse remarry?

Barbara Stonehouse was John's first wife; they were married for 30 years and had three kids. She was devastated by John's apparent death on a beach in Miami, Florida, and was surprised when it emerged that it had all been faked. Barbara and John divorced in 1978 after he was convicted on fraud charges.

Did John Stonehouse remarry? After his divorce from his first wife, Barbara Stonehouse, John married Sheila Buckley, his assistant, while still a sitting member of parliament. They lived together until he died in 1988 and had one child.
