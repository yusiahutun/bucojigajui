7 natural alternatives to black hair dye without chemicals

Publish date: 2024-07-01

Are you bothered by the presence of white or grey hair on your head? For many people, white hair is a problem since it is usually associated with old age. But did you know that there are several natural alternatives to black hair dye without chemicals that would help you achieve uniformly black hair?

How can I make my hair black without dye? For a long time, many people have preferred chemical hair dyes to get rid of white hair. However, lately, the trend is gradually changing as most people are becoming aware of the negative impacts of the chemical dyes on the skin.

Furthermore, with natural dyes, you do not need to pay the expensive service fee charged after applying chemical dyes at a beauty parlour. The procedures for using natural dye are simple, and you can do it all alone as a DIY.

What is the best natural black hair dye? If you have struggled to eradicate white or grey hair for a long time, then this question must have probably crossed your mind severally. While you will come across a range of artificial dyes in various shops, some of them relatively cheap, it is not advisable to use them.

It would be best if you opted for homemade black hair dyes that are better than the artificial ones. Therefore, if you wonder whether there are natural ways to dye hair black, then here are a myriad of options you can use without much hustle:

1. Coffee

For most people, coffee appears to be one of their favourite beverages, yet it is also a natural hair dye. Here is a simple procedure on how to colour hair black naturally using coffee:

  • Brew ground coffee and then allow it to cool for approximately thirty minutes.
  • Dip your hair into the coffee for a few minutes.
  • Wash and rinse your hair with clean water and then apply conditioner. Repeat the process severally to have lovely dark hair.

2. Black tea

Black tea works similar to coffee but its effect is not long-lasting. You should repeat the procedure severally to maintain the black colour. Here are the steps:

  • Wash your hair thoroughly before soaking it in black tea for at least 15 minutes.
  • Repeat the process after every 15 minutes as many times as you can.

The more you do the procedure, the darker your hair gets. When you are done with the process, shampoo and condition your hair and then pat dry with a towel.

3. Henna powder

It is recommendable to wear gloves when applying the henna paste on your hair because it stains. Here are the steps to follow:

  • Prepare a henna paste using henna powder and water.
  • Start spreading the paste from the tip of your hair as you proceed towards the roots.
  • When you are sure that the entire hair is covered with henna paste, cover it with a shower cap for about an hour. Later, rinse, shampoo, and condition the hair.

4. Cocoa

Cocoa, also a beverage, proves to be an essential natural alternative to black hair dye. Use these simple steps to make the cocoa paste:

  • Add a little water to cocoa powder to form a viscous paste, and gently rub the paste into your hair until your hair is well covered.
  • Wrap your hair with a towel or cover with a shower cap for at least thirty minutes or more.
  • Clean your hair with water and condition it for the best results.

5. Mustard oil

When handling mustard oil, caution must be taken. Ideally, it would help if put on protective gear as it is highly staining. Since you do not want the mustard oil to get into contact with your skin, it is advisable to apply petroleum jelly along your hairline, neck region, and ears. use the following steps to prepare mustard oil:

6. Sage herbs

Many people know about the medicinal benefits of the herb. However, it is also useful if you want to darken your hair without opting for chemicals.

  • Prepare natural black hair dye by adding one cup of sage to a water container and then boil for a couple of hours.
  • Allow the solution to cool, and then put your hair in it for approximately half an hour.
  • Wash and condition your hair. If you do not achieve the desired dark hair can repeat the procedure until a few days until you are satisfied.

7. Amla powder

Amla powder is prominently known for treating diarrhoea, jaundice, and several other health complications, but lately, it is gaining popularity as a natural alternative to black hair dye.
