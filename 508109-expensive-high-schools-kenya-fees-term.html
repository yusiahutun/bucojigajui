Most expensive high schools in Kenya: top 10 list

The most expensive high schools in Kenya charge mind-boggling amounts of cash in fees. These institutions are equipped with world-class teaching and learning facilities.

Most use international curricula, but some choose borrow aspects of the Kenyan curriculum as well. Discover the most expensive international schools in Kenya below.

10. St. Andrews Turi - KES 1,128,000 pa (tuition & boarding)

St. Andrews Turi has consistently featured on the list of the most expensive secondary schools in Kenya. It is an international boarding school located in Nakuru near Molo town. St. Andrews Turi offers a British curriculum for preparatory and A-level students.

St. Andrews Turi fees are KES 963,000 for year nine students, KES 1,043,000 for years 10 and 11, and KES 1,128,000 per year for year 12 and 13 students (boarding category).

9. Light International School - KES 2,016,000 pa (tuition only)

Light International School is an international school in Kenya with five branches across Nairobi, Mombasa, and Malindi. The termly tuition fees at Light International School is KES 588,000 for year 11 and KES 672,000 for years 12 and 13. The boarding fee is KES 395,000 per term.

8. Woodland Star International School - $17,490 pa (≈KES 2.2 million) pa (tuition only)

Woodland Star International School is a small, progressive international school in Kenya. The institution is located in the emerald tea fields of Tigoni. The annual tuition fee for self-paying people is $17,490 or $5,830 per term for students in grades nine and above.

7. Hillcrest International School - KES 2,372,100 pa (tuition only)

Hillcrest is an international school with a large diversity of cultures and backgrounds. Hillcrest is is built on a philosophy of inclusion, cooperation and mutual respect for each other. The annual tuition fee for year nine is KES 2,270,400, KES 2,452,200 for year 10, KES 2,452,200 for year 12, and between KES 1,929,000 and KES 2,590,800 for years 12 and 13.

6. Braeburn School - KES 2,395,800 pa (tuition only)

Braeburn School is a co-educational international school in Nairobi. The institution teaches children aged three to 18, following the National Curriculum of England and Wales. Braeburn School fees (tuition fee) is 2,395,800 pa for years 12 and 13, KES 2,239,200 for year 11, KES 2,239,200 for year 10, and KES 2,100,000 for year nine.

5. Greensteds International School - KES 2.6 million pa (tuition & boarding)

Greensteds International School is located about six kilometres from Nakuru. The institution is a co-educational day and boarding school that was established in 1936.

The institution offers education based on an adapted form of the National Curriculum of England from Early Years to IGCSE. The termly fees at Greensteds International School is KES 815,500 pa for years 10 and 11, and KES 895,000 for year 12 students. This means the annual fee for year 12 students is about KES 2.6 million.

4. Peponi School - KES 3.8 million pa (tuition & boarding)

Peponi School is located off Thika Road on Northlands City. It was established in 1989 and offers a rich and rewarding curriculum for pupils. The school offers education based on the English National Curriculum for England and Wales.

The termly fees at Peponi School for years nine to 13 is KES 1,288,035, leading to about KES 3.8 million pa for full boarding students.

3. Brookhouse School - KES 3.9 million pa (tuition only)

Brookhouse School is a private, international school located in Nairobi. The school has two branches located in the leafy suburbs of Karen and Runda. It was established in 1981 and offers learning based on the British curriculum.

Brookhouse School fees per term (tuition only) is KES 830,000 for year nine, KES 850,000 for year 10, KES 1,275,000 for year 11, KES 870,000 for year 12, and KES 1,305,000 for year 13. There are three terms a year in Brookhouse School.

2. International School of Kenya (ISK) - $36,600 (≈ KES 4.8 million) pa (tuition only)

The International School of Kenya is the most expensive private school in Nairobi. ISK is a private, non-profit school offering pre-kindergarten to grade 12 educational programs. The institution was established in 1976 through a joint partnership between the United States and Canadian governments.

Grades 9 to 12 at ISK are the equivalent of high school in Kenya. ISK school fees is $35,200 (about KES 4.6 million) for grades 9 and 10 and $36,600 (about KES 4.8 million) for grades 11 and 12. Note that this amount covers tuition fees only.

1. German School Nairobi - €40,122 (≈KES 5.62 million) pa (tuition only)

The German School Nairobi is a community school where girls and boys can acquire their secondary school diploma or the German International Abitur. The termly tuition fees for grades five to 12 is €13,374 or about KES 1,874,008. The annual tuition amount is €40,122 (KES 5.62 million).
