19 Surprisingly Chic Overall Outfits

Publish date: 2024-02-17

Overalls were once associated with farmers and small children as protective wear, but the single utilitarian garment has come a long way. The denim one-piece has become ubiquitous in Hollywood, cropping up in the summer of 2023 on Selena Gomez, where the dungarees doubled as a bikini cover-up, on Blake Lively while visiting Martha Stewart in 2022, and on Jennifer Lopez, who has thoroughly embraced the style over the years in myriad iterations.

With various fits, fabrics, and styles available, it's easy to understand the resurgence of overalls: They can be worn anywhere at anytime. Throwing a denim pair over a tee or tank is easy and forgiving, but pairing a black leather style with a turtleneck or sweater and boots is just as easy, less expected, and epitomizes chic comfort.

Here, we give you 19 overall outfit ideas to suit your every need and any occasion — whether for work, school, or play.

Embrace Stripes

A striped pair does plenty of talking, so balance them out with a solid shirt underneath, then go with a classic shoe choice, such as high-top sneakers, Doc Martens, or Birkenstocks.

Layer Up

Despite practically being an outfit in themselves, overalls pair well with layers, both underneath and on top. Sweaters, turtlenecks, coats, hoodies, and scarves are all great starts, but standout shoes, hats, and bags give the look added complexity and style.

Go for Texture

The word "overalls" may instantly conjure thoughts of denim, but there's a wonderful world of luxe materials to experiment with, including bouclé, shearling, satin, and suede, to start. Pair them with basics like a crisp white button-down for an elegant take, or try a contrasting patterned shirt underneath for a bespoke feel.

Channel Cottagecore

A plaid, checked, or gingham pair of dungarees instantly channel cottagecore vibes, especially when you add a grandpa cardigan, sweet updo, and clogs. A vintage leather or fringe handbag really brings the feeling home.

Try a Leather Alternative

If you love leather jackets and pants, it's time to embrace leather overalls, too. This material continues to come across as sleek and versatile, and a dark colorway will help make your vibrant shirts, shoes, and accessories really pop.

Show Some Skin

If your goal is to beat the heat, skip a full shirt in favor of a bralette or sports bra, which will work just as well. A wide-leg, baggy style keeps the look feeling casual and curated, whether you finish it with a pointed heel or a chunky sneaker.

Take on a Cropped Style

A cropped variation can prove super versatile, working well with a blouse or a tee but also pairing well with heels, sandals, and knee-high boots.

Tuck Into Boots

Show off your tall boots and take on an equestrian-chic look by tucking overalls (a dark pair is especially successful here) into a tall pair of boots. A blazer or patterned jacket makes the look extra polished. 

Go (Super) Big

An extra roomy silhouette can be just as stylish as a more fitted pair. Let the shape take center stage by pairing the overalls with a simple fitted tee and choose one flashy accessory like a statement bag or platform shoes.

Try Contrast Stitching

Contrast stitching is a great way to jump on the '90s trend via your dungarees. Add an oversized vintage or concert tee and chunky dad sneakers or high-tops to really bring the look to life.

Pair Them with a Hoodie

Your favorite hoodie will be just as at home with your overalls as your favorite tee, with the added benefit of even more coziness. A pair of classic sneakers makes the comfortable ensemble look intentional.

Make a Statement with Sleeves

Perhaps no item begs for fun sleeves like overalls do. A blouse or sweater with balloon sleeves will pop against a solid color pair of overalls, whether you complete the look with casual sneakers or more formal footwear.

Offset with Strappy Heels

Overalls saw renewed interest when Sarah Jessica Parker wore them on the first season of And Just Like That... paired with strappy platform sandals. The key to nailing the Carrie-approved look is keeping the rest of the outfit simple with a plain tee or button down and letting the contrasting look shine.

Make It a Suit

Turning your overalls into a suit with a matching jacket adds instant formality without making the look stuffy. Go the extra mile with a shirt and tie or scarf, but keep the look playful with chunky loafers.

Go Preppy

Jeans and loafers are a match made in heaven, so it's no stretch to make the leap to embracing prep with overalls. Add a pair of delicate socks to your loafers or a sweet pair of Mary Janes, and style the entire look with a blouse and classic structured handbag.

Rock All-White Everything

A head-to-toe white look is always chic, and it's no different where overalls are concerned. A sheer blouse and strappy sandals look especially put together, but a white sweater in a luxe fabric and heeled boots would deliver the same effect in colder weather.

Add a Belt

Many of the styling tips that work for long dresses can be equally effective with overalls, and adding a belt is one of them. In addition to defining the waist, a belt can also break up a solid color pair of overalls, and a simple belt with metallic buckle will dress up a basic pair of dungarees.

Double-Up on Denim

On chillier days, you can always style your dungarees with sweaters or trench coats, but a denim jacket is an unexpected, monochromatic pick that will help make this staple to feel more fashion-forward.

Show Off with Cutouts

Whether you leave them bare to show some skin or not, cutouts are a great detail that add interest to your overalls and make your look perfect for a night out. Pair with heels and a petite handbag for an evening look, or top with a knit cardi for a more demure look.
