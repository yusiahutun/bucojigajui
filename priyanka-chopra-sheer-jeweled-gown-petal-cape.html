See Priyanka Chopra In a Sheer Jeweled Gown with a Petal-Adorned Cape

Publish date: 2024-03-15

Priyanka Chopra's latest red carpet look is a luxurious spin on naked dressing.

The Citadel actress and her husband Nick Jonas attended the inauguration of the Cultural Centre (NMACC) in Mumbai, India last night, where Chopra wore a head-turning jeweled gown.

The ensemble was composed of a semi-sheer gown with a sweetheart neckline and a gauzy bodice, with sparkling jewels completely covering the torso and fading down the flowy skirt. The dress was paired with a matching cape featuring silk petal appliqués adorning the shoulder and wrapping around the high collar.

Her accessories included gold ankle-strap platform heels, diamond stud earrings. and a matching pendant bracelet. Her dark hair was pulled into an elegant updo, and her glam included a smoky winged eye and a mauve lip.

SUJIT JAISWAL//Getty Images

Meanwhile, the Jonas Brother wore a sleek black suit featuring a matching shirt with a peek-a-boo lace design at the collar.

SUJIT JAISWAL//Getty Images

During a recent appearance on Dax Shepard's podcast, Armchair Expert, the Quantico star spoke candidly on first communicating with Jonas in 2016 and waiting until May 2018 for their first official date (though they did famously pose for photos together at the 2017 Met Gala). She also opened up about her past relationships.

"In relationships, I kept making the same mistakes. I was with similar guys [who were] a little bit narcissistic. I just needed to figure out what about me evoked that. Like, why did I attract it?" she said. "A lot of the patterns in my relationships was my feeling gaslit. I gave that kind of power to the people that I was with, where I was like, 'You come first.' … I always looked for someone who I could be vulnerable with, never realizing my vulnerability gave this incredible power to my partner."

She continued, "I've had wonderful relationships, and my exes were bad, good, ugly, but they shaped who I am at that time. But this last one and the year and a half that I took before I met my husband officially gave me so much clarity on what I needed, not what I wanted."
