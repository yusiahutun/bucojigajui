Shakira's mother hospitalised in Barcelona 'as a matter of urgency' after blood clot found

Publish date: 2024-08-07

Shakira's mum has reportedly been hospitalised in Barcelona after a blood clot was found in her leg.

Nidia Ripoll was initially said to have suffered a stroke.

But Spanish media, citing sources close to Shakira understood to have spoken to American Spanish-language TV broadcaster Telemundo, were saying this afternoon her health problem was related to deep vein thrombosis and had not affected her brain.

There has not yet been any comment from the Colombian singer-songwriter, whose dad William Mubarak's health following an operation last year has been linked to her decision to delay her move to Miami with her two children following her split with former footballer Gerard Pique.

The 46-year-old was expected to leave the Catalan capital for Florida at the start of the year with sons Milan, ten, and Sasha, eight, after signing a child custody agreement with the ex-Barcelona defender.

Shakira's mum Nidia Ripoll (right) has reportedly been hospitalised in Barcelona. They are pictured with her father (left)

The health of Shakira's father - William Mubarak - following an operation last year has been linked to her decision to delay her move to Miami with her two children following her split with former footballer Gerard Pique (second from left)

Doctors reportedly detected Shakira's mum's health problem after she felt unwell last week and sought medical attention.

Telemundo and Spanish press are saying she was admitted to hospital last Friday as a matter of urgency, but is now 'out of danger' and is expected to be allowed to go home shortly.

It is not yet clear if it will lead to new delays in Shakira's plans to leave Spain with her children.

After postponing her January move, she was expected to make the switch at the end of the school year in June.

But Shakira expert Laura Fa, one of two journalists who regularly report on the mum-of-two for Catalan newspaper El Periodico, said earlier this month the artist was looking at a new date of April 1.

Shakira and Pique confirmed in November they had signed a child custody agreement after a protracted negotiation which came to a head with a marathon 12-hour meeting with their lawyers. They signed the accord at a court in Barcelona in December.

As part of the agreement the retired Barcelona defender, now dating 23-year-old Catalan beauty Clara Chia, will spend holiday time with his children.

Shakira's wish to move to Miami where she used to live and part of her family are based is understood to have been the principal sticking point in the former couple's previous failure to strike an out-of-court settlement.

The pair, who split last May after more than a decade together, said in their joint statement: 'We wish to communicate that we have signed an agreement that guarantees the welfare of our children and that will be ratified in court as part of a mere formal procedure.

'Our sole objective is to provide them with the upmost security and protection, and we trust that their privacy will be respected.

'We appreciate the interest shown and hope that the children can continue with their lives with the necessary privacy in a safe and calm environment.'

Shakira's 91-year-old dad was hospitalised in Barcelona last year after a fall.

Shakira shared a picture on Instagram of her mother kissing her father while he was in hospital last year

He spent Christmas with his daughter in the Catalan capital after being released from the private Teknon Medical Centre in November and being put under 24/7 professional at-home care.

Shakira performed her diss track with Argentinian DJ Bizarrap, in which she fires poisoned darts at Pique and his new girlfriend, on the Jimmy Fallon late-night show just over a week ago. She went on to talk about her break-up with the former footballer.

Pique said in an interview at the weekend with Spanish newspaper El Pais he was 'very happy' following the dramatic changes in his life which have included the split with Shakira and retirement from football.

He also shut down a question about Shakira in the interview but added: 'Each parent has the responsibility to try to do the best for their children.

'It's about protecting them. That's the work of all parents with their children. That's what I'm focused on and that's my job as a father.'
