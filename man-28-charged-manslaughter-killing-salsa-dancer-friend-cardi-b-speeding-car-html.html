Man, 28, charged with manslaughter for 'killing salsa dancer and friend of Cardi B' in speeding car

Publish date: 2024-05-30

A man was charged with two counts of manslaughter for allegedly killing a salsa dancer and Cardi B's friend when he ran a red light at 50mph and caused a car to crash into them.  

Leandro Diaz Ramirez, 28, was behind the wheel of the speeding BMW when he crashed into a Subaru - which then crushed the two pedestrians in Inwood, New York.   

Joel Adames, 31, and David Fernandez, 40, were killed in the August 3 crash - with artist Cardi B, who was a pal of the deceased, leading the tributes and memorializing Adames.

Earlier that evening, Fernandez cut his friend Adames' hair, and the pair had gone for a drink before walking home on West 207th Street and Sherman Avenue around 4am.

The BMW Ramirez was speeding at 59mph north on Sherman Avenue when it collided with a Subaru Forester SUV headed in the opposite direction at the West 207th Street intersection, police said. 

He sped through a red right moments before the fatal crash. Ramirez was driving over double the speed limit.  

The impact launched the SUV onto the sidewalk, where it slammed into and crushed the two men. They died from their injuries earlier this year.   

The driver broke several ribs and his pelvis.

Ramirez has now been indictment by the New York State Supreme Court with two counts of manslaughter of in the second degree, one count of assault in the second degree, two counts of criminally negligent homicide and three counts of assault in the third degree. 

District Attorney Alvin L. Bragg Jr,. said: 'As alleged, this reckless speeding and driving ended in tragedy.

'Manhattanites should not be killed by drivers when they are simply walking in their neighborhood. We will continue to protect the safety of pedestrians and drivers on our streets.'
