The Hendricks family murder case: What happened and latest updates

Publish date: 2024-05-25

A mother and her children were murdered, and their bodies badly mutilated. The man of the house was away on a business trip. He was the first suspect and was sentenced to life imprisonment but was later released. So, what happened to The Hendricks family?

The night of November 7th, 1983, will remain a day to remember for American businessman David Hendricks. That night, he lost his lovely wife, Susan, and three children in their house along 313 Carl Drive. Here is everything you need to know about what transpired.

The Hendricks family

The family consisted of David and Susan Hendricks. The couple had three children, Rebekah, Grace, and Benjamin. David was born in Morton Grove but was raised in Oak Park, Illinois.

David Hendricks is an American businessman. He started and ran a highly successful business in Bloomington, Illinois. He sold an orthopaedic back brace he had patented and was known as a staple of the Bloomington community.

David Hendricks family were members of a strict fundamentalist section of Protestantism, Plymouth Brethren. The household was run in accordance with its teachings. They mostly kept to themselves and spent most of their time with family or members of the group.

The family lived an arguably great life until November 7th, 1983. Then, Susan and her children were found murdered lying in their bed. On one of the children's beds, there was an axe and a butcher knife.

What happened to the Hendricks family?

The afternoon of November 7th, 1983, started like any other day for the Hendricks. David went about his business as usual while Susan spent time with the children. He came back in the evening, allowing Susan to go for a baby shower in Delevan.

In the evening, David took the children to their favourite restaurant, Chuck E. Cheese. They took food and drinks and played games until 8.00 P.M. when they left for home. By 9.30 P.M., the children were in their beds, asleep.

Susan Hendricks returned at around 10:30 P.M. and spent time with her husband before going to bed 30 minutes later. David then left the house after 10.00 P.M. for a business trip. Later, he would check into a hotel in Wisconsin and spend the next day selling his back brace.

Before he starts returning home, he calls his wife. Unfortunately, the call is not answered. He calls again in the afternoon, but this attempt fails too. He then asks his assistant to try out the neighbour if they can reach out and knock at his door.

David got worried and made a call to the Bloomington Police Department. As a result, officer Mike Hibbens and detective Dennis O’Brien were sent to the house to check on Susan and the children. Unfortunately, they discovered they were dead.

Hendricks crime scene

The Hendricks family murder scene was quite strange. When the police and neighbours searched Hendricks's house, they found the mutilated bodies of Susan and three children. They had been hacked to death with an axe and a butcher knife.

There was little sign of forced entry or struggle. Additionally, even though the killings were brutal, the used weapons had been cleaned and placed next to the bodies.

Arrest and prosecution of David Henricks

When David Henricks returned later that day, the police questioned him and checked for bloodstains on his clothes. However, since there was no other lead, the police decided the examine his story. After studying the children's bodies, medical examiners concluded that David's story did not fit.

The examiners pointed out issues with the children's last meal. Usually, the food leaves the stomach and moves into the small intestine within two hours. However, in all three children, vegetarian pizza toppings, which they had eaten that evening, were still in their stomachs.

Investigators estimated their time of death to be sometime around 9:30 P.M. Hendricks was still at home. He was charged with murdering his family, but the police lacked a concrete motive for such actions.

Who killed the Hendricks family?

The jury found Hendricks guilty of four counts of murder after nine weeks of trial. David was sentenced to life imprisonment on December 21st, 1988. He appealed that case, but the appeals were denied.

Journalist Steve Vogel, who followed the case closely, decided to publish a book titled Reasonable Doubt. In the book, he asked questions that many people were afraid to try. When the Supreme Court of Illinois announced a review of the case, many versions of the book were published.

Fortunately, in 1991, the Supreme court of Illinois retried the case. After seven weeks of trials, the jury found him not guilty. Therefore, David was released after serving three years in jail.
