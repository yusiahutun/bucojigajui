Stoke City is a professional football club based in Stoke-on-Trent, England.

Founded in 1863, Stoke City is one of the oldest football clubs in the world.

The club’s nickname is “The Potters.”

This nickname is derived from Stoke-on-Trent’s historical association with the pottery industry.

The stadium has a capacity of over 30,000 and provides a lively atmosphere for the team’s matches.

FAQs

1. When was Stoke City FC established?

Stoke City FC was established in 1863, making it one of the oldest football clubs in England.

2. What is Stoke City’s home stadium?

Stoke City plays their home games at the Bet365 Stadium, formerly known as the Britannia Stadium.

3. Has Stoke City ever won the English Premier League?

No, Stoke City has never won the English Premier League. Their highest league finish came in the 1935-1936 season when they finished as runners-up.

4. Who is Stoke City’s all-time leading goalscorer?

Stoke City’s all-time leading goalscorer is John Ritchie, who scored 176 goals for the club between 1962 and 1975.

5. Has Stoke City ever won any major trophies?

Yes, Stoke City has won the Football League Cup on one occasion, in the 1971-1972 season.

6. Which teams are considered Stoke City’s biggest rivals?

Stoke City’s biggest rivals are considered to be Port Vale, a club situated just a few miles away in Stoke-on-Trent.

7. How many times has Stoke City competed in European competitions?

Stoke City has competed in European competitions twice, participating in the UEFA Cup in the 1972-1973 and 2011-2012 seasons.

8. What is the nickname of Stoke City?

Stoke City is commonly known as “The Potters” due to the city’s historic connection with the pottery industry.

9. Who is the current manager of Stoke City?

The current manager of Stoke City is Michael O’Neill, who took charge of the team in November 2019.

10. How many times has Stoke City been relegated from the Premier League?

Stoke City has been relegated from the Premier League three times, in the 1997-1998, 2004-2005, and 2017-2018 seasons.
