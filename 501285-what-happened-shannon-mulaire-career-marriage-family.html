What happened to Shannon Mulaire? Career, marriage, and family

Publish date: 2024-02-16

Shannon Mulaire is an American reporter and news anchor. She is famous for having worked at NBC 10 Boston, where she was one of the station's top anchors, presenting newscasts during 4, 6, and 11 PM slots. Before NBC, Mulaire worked at Fox 25 (WFXT-TV) as a morning and weekend news anchor.

Shannon Mulaire raised eyebrows when she left her job at NBC 10. But why did the news anchor leave her job, and where is she now? Here is everything about Mulaire and her current whereabouts.

Early life

The news anchor was born in Stamford, Connecticut, on October 24, 1981. She has not shared much about her background, especially her parents and siblings. However, Mulaire revealed that her paternal grandfather was a U.S. Armed Forces veteran and a surgeon.

She also disclosed that she grew up playing soccer and would have chosen sports as a career field if she were not in journalism. Shannon attended Phillips Exeter Academy, a prestigious private high school in Exeter, New Hampshire.

She graduated from high school in 1999, then joined Wellesley College, pursuing a Bachelor of Arts in Spanish and Cinema Media Studies. She then enrolled at Emerson College, earning her master's degree in Broadcast Journalism.

Career

After finishing college, Mulaire joined WFTX TV station in (Fox 4) Fort Myers, Florida, in 2006. She worked at the station for two years as a writer and producer. She then moved to WTVT Tampa as a general assignment reporter in 2008. She also worked as a fill-in news anchor during her stint at WTVT.

In 2010, Shannon returned to the Fox network, but this time in Massachusetts. She joined Fox 25 WFXT-TV as a general assignment reporter, where she mainly did field reporting. After about a year at Fox 25, she was promoted to a weekend anchor.

Some of Mulaire's top achievement in this role includes covering the 2011 Stanley Championship Parade and assisting Fox 25 in being the first station in Boston to report on Osama Bin Landen's killing. In 2012, she was again promoted at Fox 25, becoming the morning news anchor. She was primarily involved in delivering morning news and interviewing guests during the segment.

She is also credited for helping to increase ratings and bringing in more audience for the TV station. In 2016, Shannon moved to Boston as a news anchor for NBC News (NBC 10 Boston). She presented news during the 4, 6, and 11 PM briefings. She also helped research and create stories, conducted guest interviews, and did sports coverage.

One of her significant achievements was the moderation of the Boston Mayoral Race debate hosted by NBC 10. Unfortunately, in 2022, Mulaire left NBC10 under mysterious circumstances. After taking several months off work, she joined Nickerson, a public relations company, as its director of Media relations.

What happened to Shannon Mulaire?

Mulaire was with NBC10 until July 2022, when she stopped appearing as an anchor and was replaced by Priscilla Casper. Apart from announcing the hiring of the new team, WBTS (the parent company) did not comment on her exit, nor did NBC 10.

She remained quiet about her departure, leading many people to speculate that she had been fired or forced out. The only update to the situation was on Shannon Mulaire's Instagram account, where she stated that she was "enjoying some downtime" in response to a fan asking her what was happening.
