Jute$ Career And Songs

Jute$ is a Toronto-based punk/rap artist. He came from film studies and basketball background and moved on to studying music and creating music on his own time.

He has a number of EPs under his belt, including 2020’s Overrated. His most recent song, Hollywood Hillbilly, is the lead single from his new album, which will be released in October. The following single from Jute$ will be released on October 1st, 2022.

Top songs from the early 2020 album include the classics Sideshow and Start Over, which combine trap beats with edgy lyrics to evoke the rock culture of the early 2000s. His second album, A Really Bad Dream, debuted not long after this first album.

The Nuance Magazine said that Jutes’ style is the same, but he uses more experimental techniques. This music hinted at what his fans may expect in his future music projects. He previously signed to Capitol Records but he rescinded his contract with the record label to pursue a career as an independent artist.

In July 2022, when the song Substance dropped, the red-haired artist made his professional relationship with Lovato public for the first time. Calling it “one of the fav songs I’ve ever worked on,” Jute$ wrote on Instagram about the experience. “I remember thinking wtf am I supposed to do in a Demi Lovato session,” he questioned. “Only to show up and realize she was making one of the hardest rock projects I’ve ever heard.”

Moreover, the music video for Jute$’s song Funeral, dropped in October 2021, made numerous religious undertones. You can find him on Spotify, SoundCloud, and YouTube. Some of his popular songs are “BOY WITH A BROKEN HEART,” “Give u a Call,” and “Say it Out Loud”.

Besides music, he is also on a mission to become substance-free in 2022. On 18 May 2022, he celebrated being 30 days sober. He added, “[The] longest I’ve gone since high school. Alcohol was my last remaining vice I used to deal [with] my anxiety and mental s***. Took me a minute to get here but doing my best to face s*** in a less harmful way these days.”
