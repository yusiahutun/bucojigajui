How to get your KCSE certificate: requirements and download guide

Publish date: 2024-02-08

The KCSE certificate is one of the critical documents that Form Four leavers must produce before joining any tertiary institution or getting a job. The Kenya National Examination Council (KNEC) announces the release of the documents once a national exam is complete, after which students can organize to collect them.

Academic certificates represent an educational milestone and prove that a student has attained a particular accreditation. Today, some of these documents can be acquired online, while others require you to visit the institution where you studied. So, how can you get your KCSE certificate?

How to get your KCSE certificate

Once you finish your Form Four exams, the next step is to prepare to start your career or join an institution of higher learning. Several documents are a must-have, including your KCPE and KCSE academic and leaving certificates.

Once the examinations are done and the marking complete, the national examination body, KNEC, releases these documents to the various institutions. The body then announces the dispatch of the certificates through mass media and county education offices for students, parents or guardians to collect them from their respective high schools.

KCSE certificate download

Several netizens have wondered, ''Can I get the KCSE certificate online?'' Unfortunately, you cannot download your KCSE certificate online. You can only acquire it from your high school. However, students can download other documents like the result slip on the KNEC portal.

How to replace a lost KCSE certificate

Sometimes, you may lose your document due to unavoidable circumstances. Thankfully, you can replace it by contacting the Kenya National Examination Council. So, how much does it cost to replace a KCSE certificate?

To get a new certificate, you will part with a fee of KShs 5,800 (inclusive of 16% VAT), which you should deposit into any KNEC account at Standard Chartered Bank, Cooperative National Bank, and (NBK) Kenya Commercial Bank (KCB). Here are the steps you should follow to acquire a new document:

  • Fill out an application form for a replacement certificate and submit it to the Council Secretary or Chief Executive of KNEC. The address is P. O. Box 73598 - 002000, City Square, Nairobi. You can get the forms in the KNEC offices or on the council's website.
  • The application should be attached to a letter from the high school to prove that the student had an authentic KCSE certificate from a recognized institution. If you sat your exam in a private school, or if your school closed down, you should acquire the letter from the PDE (Provincial Director of Education) or DEO (District Education Officer).
  • Once you lose this critical document, you should report the matter to the police. They will then issue a police abstract, which you should attach to the other documents to prove that you lost the original copy.
  • Once you make the payment, attach the deposit slip to the application form before submission.

    Collection of the new certificate

    After completing the above process, you can collect the certificate within sixty days from the requested date. As you wait, you can check the status of your pending certificate by login into the KNEC Query Management Information System (QMIS).

    To collect the renewed document, you must appear in person to collect the certificate. In addition, you must carry your original national identity card or passport. If you have changed your name, you must attach the necessary paperwork.

    KNEC only issues replacement certificates once per individual. You must collect the new document within two years; otherwise, it will be disposed of without consultation.

    The process of getting the KCSE certificate is straightforward. Additionally, you can collect your certificate years after completing your examination. However, it would help if you did so without delay to avoid inconveniences with prospective employers or educational institutions.
