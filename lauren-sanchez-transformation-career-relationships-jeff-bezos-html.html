Inside Lauren Sanchez's $165 billion makeover: How Jeff Bezos' fiancee transformed herself into a Ho

Publish date: 2024-07-22

Lauren Sanchez was once a budding TV star who was desperate to break into the industry after suffering from an undiagnosed learning disability for years and being turned away from her dream job at The View.

Now, she's one of the most well-known faces on the planet, often mingling with Hollywood's biggest A-listers and attending the most exclusive events alongside her billionaire Amazon founder fiancé, Jeff Bezos, 59, who is worth approximately $167 billion. 

Just this week, the journalist, 53, landed the cover of one of the most coveted magazines in the world of fashion - Vogue - and was spotted celebrating her engagement to Jeff among her vast group of celebrity pals, including Kim Kardashian, Kris Jenner, Jessica Alba, and Robert Pattinson.

So how did go from a struggling reporter to a starlet who mingles with Tinseltown's most elite? While her romance with Jeff certainly cemented her status, Lauren's rise to the top actually started long before they met.

Ever since her relationship with Jeff Bezos was exposed, Lauren Sanchez has become a top Hollywood star - mingling with other A-listers and attending exclusive events

New Mexico-based Lauren longed to be a successful newscaster from early on, but thanks to her struggle with dyslexia, she decided to give up on that dream and instead pursue a career in aviation after finishing high school.

She moved to LA to apply for a job as a stewardess - but was left heartbroken when she was turned away and told she was 'too big' to be a flight attendant, despite only weighing 121 pounds.

But a determined Lauren used her devastation as fuel, and when she turned her focus back to journalism after that, she quickly rose through the ranks. She started at a small news outlet in Phoenix, Arizona, before moving to Extra, followed by Fox Sports Net and Good Day LA. 

Thanks to her vivacious personality and flourishing job in the media, Lauren began slowly integrating herself into the celebrity world. 

Before meeting Jeff, she was romantically linked to a slew of other stars, including an actor, two famous athletes, and a top CEO of a major talent agency - but it was when her whirlwind affair with Jeff came to light in 2019 that she truly became the subject of the public's interest.

It was revealed that they were dating while they were both married to other people, which ultimately led to them leaving their respective partners - and the scandalous start to their romance only propelled Lauren into mega-stardom even more.

It's only natural that the world became fiercely fascinated with the woman who stole the heart of one of the richest men on the globe, especially since the couple was ultimately able to overcome the explosive cheating scandal and the accusations of blackmail and extortion that shrouded the start of their romance.

After four years of lavish getaways, private jet rides, dinner dates at the most sought-after restaurants in the world, designer shopping trips, yacht rides, and invites to parties amongst the top stars, Jeff and Lauren became engaged back in May.

As the couple gear up to walk down the aisle together and Lauren continues to make waves in the media, FEMAIL went ahead and recapped her journey from start to finish - from her roller-coaster quest to find love, which included a failed marriage and multiple failed engagements, to her impressive takeover of Hollywood.

 

Lauren's rise to the spotlight: After being told she was 'too big' to be a stewardess at age 18, she set out on becoming a journalist - but was left 'devastated' when she was turned down from The View

Lauren grew up in New Mexico, and dreamed of being a journalist from a young age - but because she suffered from undiagnosed dyslexia, she began to believe that she couldn't do it 

Lauren grew up in Albuquerque, New Mexico, and dreamed of being a journalist from a young age - but because she suffered from undiagnosed dyslexia throughout her childhood, she began to believe that she couldn't do it.

Instead, she decided she wanted to become a flight attendant, and at age 18, she moved to LA where she applied for a job as a Southwest Airlines stewardess - but was turned away because she was told she weighed too much. She is seen in the late 80s

Instead, she decided she wanted to become a flight attendant, and at age 18, after finishing high school, she moved to Los Angeles, California, where she applied for a job as a Southwest Airlines stewardess.

However, the then-teenager, who weighed just over 120 pounds, told Wall Street Journal earlier this year that she was forced to stand on a scale during the interview process - a standard practice for flight attendants at the time - and she ultimately didn't get the job because she was told she was too big.

'Back then, they weighed you, and I weighed 121 pounds. They said, "You need to be 115,"' she recalled.

Unsure of where to turn, a heartbroken Lauren decided to go to back to school, and enrolled at El Camino College in Torrance, California. 

It was there that she rediscovered her love for journalism after a teacher encouraged her to get tested for a learning disability and she finally realized that she was suffering from dyslexia.

'[The teacher said], "You're not dumb. You're just dyslexic. Let me give you tools on how to write,"' she explained, adding that the professor gifted her a pocket dictionary to keep with her at all times.

'It changed my life. I went from barely a 2.0 student to the dean's list and got a scholarship to USC.'

Lauren transferred to University of Southern California in 1992, which she attended for two years while working as a desk assistant at the LA-based news company KCOP-TV.

In 1994, she left to school to pursue her career as a reporter full time after she was offered a job at KTVK in Phoenix, Arizona.

She said she had bonded with creator Barbara during the audition process, who personally called her up herself to deliver the sad news herself, which resulted in her crying 'for days.' The position was ultimately given to Lisa Ling.

But according to Lauren, the advice that Barbara gave her throughout the process was something that stuck with her for years.

'She really helped me with my career. Not only as someone I looked up to, but really guided me when I was up for The View,' she revealed to WSJ.

'Producers were trying to make me dress extra conservative and she saw me and said, "What happened?"

'She said, "They will try and make you ordinary. Don't let them. Then, if you fail, at least you fail as yourself." I never forgot that.'

She's also working on writing her first children's book called Flynn, The Fly Who Flew; recently launched another production company called Adventure & Fellowship; and is in the midst of planning an all-female mission to outer space on Bezos' aerospace manufacturer company Blue Origin for early 2024.

In addition, she got her helicopter pilot's license in 2016, and has a fierce passion for flying - something she once referred to as her 'calling.'
