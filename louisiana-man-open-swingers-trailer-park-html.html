Louisiana man to open swingers trailer park

Publish date: 2024-05-21

A Louisiana man has announced plans to open the first trailer park for swingers with the slogan 'bring your house and share your spouse'. 

David Aucoin plans to open Tee Boi’s Swingers Trailer Park in Mamou, Louisiana, next Memorial Day weekend. 

After putting up a sign announcing his plan at the proposed site, he says he has received dozens of calls from all over the country from people who say they'll come and visit.

Among facilities will be a nude swimming pool, a naked yoga 'stadium', and a 'key party cabana'. 

He recently put up the sign at the site where he says he'll build it, along with his phone number, and says people have expressed an interest.

David Aucoin plans to open Tee Boi’s Swingers Trailer Park in Mamou, Louisiana, next Memorial Day weekend. Since putting up a sign announcing his plans, he says he's received dozens of phone calls

Speaking to local media outlet KLFY, he said he plans to open the site on Memorial Day weekend 2022 

He said he was only kidding when he asked people to submit photos of their spouses 'for approval' but that he's received dozens of submissions

'You can come to Mamou for many good things. This will be one of them,' he told local network KLFY, adding the swinger scene was 'bigger' than most know.

'Live free and don’t be scared of it. There’s a big community of it. I think a bunch of them aren’t shy.

'The ones that are shy, hell with them. Roll with it. It’s a good thing compared to most things!' he said.  

Some of the people who have called him haven't believed that the plan was genuine, he said. 

'We have got some from Pennsylvania, we have got some from Arkansas, of course all around Acadiana, all around Mamou, Ville Platte, Lafayette, Baton Rouge, New Orleans, Slidell. 

Aucoin also launched a Facebook page to promote his plans

'We have text messages from all over the country. It’s mind boggling. 

'Most of them call or text just to see if it’s real, just to see if somebody is going to answer because they’ll say, "Oh, no. Nobody is there. It’s not a real number." 

'Or they’ll get on Facebook and say, "Oh, it’s not a real number."

'I got on there and said, "I’d be a fat frog’s butt if it’s not a real number."

'Everybody starts calling, and they start calling,  "Hey, what’s y’all’s address? Is this in Mamou? Where is this at?" Yes, it is.' 

He posted on his sign for prospective visitors to send photos of their spouses for 'approval'. 

He said it was intended as a joke but that people took it seriously and started sending in images. 

'That was more of a joke to kind of ease up the people and just to create a vibe.

'People went further with it than you can imagine. We’ve gotten many pictures and many phone calls and many texts, voicemails. It’s unreal,' he said.
