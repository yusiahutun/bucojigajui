'Only in Florida': Alligator strolls across busy Tampa junction after waiting for police escort

Publish date: 2024-06-08

Florida drivers faced an unexpected traffic- hold-up when an alligator interrupted traffic at a busy Tampa junction.

The huge beast is seen in video footage laying in the middle of the junction, obstructing several lanes of traffic, only to calmly stroll away when police turned up and seemingly gave it VIP treatment to cross the road. 

The alligator, estimated at 10-feet long, attempted to cross a six-lane boulevard near Interstate 75 around 1:30pm Sunday. 

Local media reported that a Hillsborough County Sheriff's deputy guided it off the road and to a nearby lake.

In a video, the huge beast appeared to be waiting for a police escort as it lay in the middle of the road, obstructing several lanes of traffic, only to calmly stroll away when officers approached

A video of the moment, shot by Ruchama Lubin-Desir, has been viewed more than 12million times. 

'It's not something I had personally seen before but it can happen all the time in Florida,' the 36-year-old said.  'Only in Florida, that's my slogan!'

Drivers were kept waiting through multiple traffic light cycles, driver Sue Blockel Juncal told McClatchy News. The disruption lasted nearly five minutes.

'He sauntered while drivers scooted around him. He'd take five steps, pause, take five more steps and pause. The honking and cars didn't seem to bother him ... like he'd done all this before,' she said.
