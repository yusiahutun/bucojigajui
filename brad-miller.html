Brad Miller | Professional Career & Injuries

The Seattle Mariners drafted Brad Miller in the second round, 62nd overall, in the 2011 MLB Draft. But, he was signed by Clinton Lumberkings, a Minor League affiliate of the Seattle Mariners.

He later played for High Desert Mavericks in the California League in 2012. He was awarded the California League’s All-Star and Player of the Week.

Apart from this, he also won Mariners Heart and Soul Award for his exemplary performance and leadership qualities on and off the field.

In 2013, Miller was called to play for Seattle Mariners in the major league and made his debut  the same day against the Chicago Cubs.

He impressed everyone with his outstanding skills. As a result, he won his first American League (AL) the Player of the Week award.

After two successful seasons with the Mariners, he was traded to Tampa Bay Rays. But, he couldn’t perform well due to his frequent injuries. He was then traded to Milwaukee Brewers in 2018.

However, his injuries hampered his gameplay even more. He had to undergo several surgeries, limiting his gameplay and performance. Eventually, he was traded to Cleveland Indians in 2019.

He later played for Philadelphia Phillies and St. Louis Cardinals and is currently playing for Texas Rangers.

Injuries

Brad Miller was sidelined several times due to his injuries. Miller missed most of the games in 2018 after suffering from a pinky toe fracture, abdominal strain, and right groin strain.

He also had hip labrum damage, which was later repaired using an arthroscopic procedure. Likewise, he went through microfracture surgery to repair cartilage damage.
