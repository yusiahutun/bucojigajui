If the shoe fits walk in it til your high heels break - analysis of long story short

Publish date: 2024-07-31

Taylor Swift's song "Long Story Short" is a masterful exploration of personal growth, emotional endurance, and the nature of conflict, both external and internal. The recurring motif of the "shoe" and other symbolic language forms a lyrical tapestry that narrates the tale of an individual's journey through tumultuous events and the consequential realization of self-worth and peace. The focus of the analysis lies particularly on the line, "If the shoe fits, walk in it 'til your high heels break," - a phrase rich with symbolism, which speaks to the overarching themes of struggle, resilience, and self-awareness. This, along with other symbolic elements and character portrayals, invokes parallels with cultural and literary tropes, leading to a deeper understanding of the lyrical narrative.

The phrase, "If the shoe fits, walk in it 'til your high heels break," is a skillful blending of idiomatic expression with vivid symbolism. The well-known idiom, "If the shoe fits, wear it," advises that if a critique applies to someone, they should accept it. However, Swift's usage elevates the phrase beyond mere acceptance to a call to endure, to persist, even when the journey—symbolized by the high heels—becomes unbearable. It speaks to the protagonist’s resilience in the face of adversity, akin to the enduring characters from iconic narratives such as Odysseus from Homer’s epic 'Odyssey', a warrior navigating a sea of trials, driven by the determination to return home.

The song features recurring images of warfare: "I tried to pick my battles 'til the battle picked me," and "Like the war of words, I shouted in my sleep." These vivid descriptions build up an image of a protagonist locked in an exhausting struggle, evoking the 'Hero's Journey' archetype, in which a protagonist ventures into the unknown, faces trials, and returns transformed. Swift's protagonist, like so many before her, endures an arduous journey, from the depths of a metaphorical battle to the triumph of her own survival.

Swift also weaves in the symbolism of falling and rising throughout the song, presenting a clear trajectory of decline and ascension. The lyrics “I fell from the pedestal” and “Pushed from the precipice” represent the downfall, resonating with the story of Icarus, who, in Greek mythology, falls from the sky due to his hubris. Yet, Swift’s protagonist survives, as indicated by “Climbed right back up the cliff.” This continual rise and fall symbolizes the cyclical nature of struggles and triumphs in life, mirroring the phoenix rising from the ashes in various mythologies.

The song captures a transformational journey, a shift from external conflicts to internal resolution. It culminates in the chorus where Swift sings, "Now I'm all about you." The "you" might be a romantic interest, but considering the context, it could also signify a new, more contented version of the protagonist herself. This sentiment is evocative of the concept of self-acceptance and love, reminding one of the transformative journeys portrayed in literary works like Alice Walker's 'The Color Purple', where the protagonist Celie discovers self-worth and love after a life of hardship.

The usage of the phrase, "Your nemeses will defeat themselves before you get the chance to swing," is a nod to the notion that personal enemies, whether real or perceived, often bring about their downfall through their own actions. This idea is a recurring trope in literature, seen in Shakespeare's 'Macbeth' where Macbeth's ambition leads to his self-destruction. Swift's lyric signifies the protagonist's understanding of this concept and her decision to stay above the fray.

Lastly, the line, "And my waves meet your shore, ever and evermore," represents the climax of the protagonist's emotional journey. It symbolizes a harmonious convergence, evoking images of endless ebb and flow—a ceaseless dance of energy. It's reminiscent of the constant tug and play between the conscious and unconscious mind, as detailed in Carl Jung's theories of personality.

Overall, "Long Story Short" is a deeply symbolic and narrative-driven song that navigates the intricate path of personal battles, endurance, and growth. Taylor Swift's lyrics draw upon familiar cultural and literary tropes, using them to articulate a timeless journey from struggle to self-awareness and peace. Like the enduring characters from iconic narratives, Swift's protagonist embodies the resilience and growth that accompany life's trials and tribulations, offering listeners a poignant exploration of human resilience and self-discovery.
