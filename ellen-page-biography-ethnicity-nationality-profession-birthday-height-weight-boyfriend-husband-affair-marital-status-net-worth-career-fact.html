Short Description of Ellen Page:

Ellen Grace Philpotts-Page, aka Ellen Page, is an actress from Canada who started her acting career through television series Pit Pony. She is also known for her roles in the series, Trailer Park Boys, and ReGenesis. Ellen has won the Austin Film Critics Association’s Award for Best Actress for her roles on 2015’s drama Hard Candy.

Early Life and Education:

Ellen Page first opened her eyes on February 21, 1987, in Halifax, Nova Scotia. She was a daughter of Martha Philpotts (a teacher) and Dennis Page (a graphic designer). She was born as Ellen Grace Philpotts-Page and raised at Halifax, Nova Scotia.

Regarding her education, she attended the Halifax Grammar School and graduated from the Shambhala School. From the Vaughan Road Academy, she studied the Interact Program.

Career:

Traveling towards her career, Ellen started her acting career in the year 1997, with the Canadian TV-movie Pit Pony. Similarly, her US TV debut was with the series, Homeless to Harvard: The Liz Murray Story in the year 2003. Page has appeared on several Canadian series, such as Trailer Park Boys, ReGenesis, and Marion Bridge.

Her career turned over after she appeared in indie thriller Hard Candy. Then she appeared on Juno, for which she received MTV Movie Award, Spirit Award, and Teen Choice Award for Best Actress Comedy and was nominated for Golden Globe, Academy Award, BAFTA, and Screen Actors Guild Award for Best Actress.

She has appeared in the movies such as X-Men: The Last Stand, The Tracey Fragments, To Rome with Love, X-Men: Days of Future Past, and My Days of Mercy.
