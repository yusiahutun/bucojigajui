Quick Facts about Sunny Anderson

Sunny posing for a click, Source: Food Network

  • Born on 9th April 1975 in Lawton, Oklahoma, the United States.
  • Grew up in the Military brat by the parents Linda Anderson and Thomas Anderson.
  • Attended Madison High School in San Antonio, Texas.
  • Graduated from the United States Air force and ranked the Senior Airman.
  • Worked in the Air Force News Agency.
  • In 1997, was discharged from the Air Force.
  • Worked as a radio personality in different radio stations including KCJZ and KONO-FM in San Antonio, WDTJ in Detriot, Michigan, WJWZ in Montogomery, Alabama and KUMX in Fort Polk, Louisiana and others.
  • Was the owner of a catering company based in Jersey City, New Jersey named Sunny's Delicious Dishes.
  • In 2005, appeared on the Food Networks.
  • Hosted How'd That Get On My Plate? and the show, Cooking for Real.
  • Co-hosted Food Network program, Gotta Get along with Marc Istook.
  • Appeared as an Editor for Food & Lifestyle for the magazine, Hip Hop Weekly.
  • Co-hosted The Kitchen, the series of Food Network along Katie Lee, Marcela Valladolid, Geoffery Zakarian and Jeff Mauro.
  • Featured as a guest chef in the shows like The Early Show, The Talk, The View and the Good Morning America.
  • Diagnosed with the ulcerative colitis.
  • Published her book named, Sunny's Kitchen: Easy Food for Real Life became one of the best seller books in the New York.
  • Has an estimated net worth of $ 5 million.
