Basil Criticos: Supreme Court Orders National Bank to Pay Ex-Taveta MP Whopping KSh 2.2b

Publish date: 2024-05-31
  • National Bank will have to pay former Taita Taveta MP Basil Criticos KSh 2.2 billion for selling his land at an undervalue 15 years ago
  • Three judges from the appellate court established that the parcel of land of approximately 15,994.5 acres in Taita Taveta was unlawfully auctioned
  • Justices Roselyn Nambuye, Wanjiru Karanja, and Patrick Kiage said that the bank undervalued the parcel of land by selling it at KSh 55 million

Nairobi - The National Bank of Kenya (NBK) has suffered a significant blow after the Supreme Court dismissed its application for review in a case involving a disputedthe piece of land.

The Bank will have to pay former Taita Taveta MP Basil Criticos KSh 2.2 billion for selling his land at an undervalued price 15 years ago.

"The Notice of Motion lacks merit as the applicants have not highlighted any issues, the determination of which would transcend the circumstances of the matter at hand to justify a review of the Court of Appeal's ruling," the court ruled.

On Friday, June 16, 2023, the judges further said:

"Neither have the applicants raised any substantial question of law, the determination of which would have a significant bearing on the public interest. Consequently, for the reasons aforesaid, we order that the Notice of Motion dated December 22, 2022, is hereby dismissed. Also, the costs of this application shall be borne by the applicants."

Supreme Courts' landmark ruling

On Friday, June 16, three judges from the appellate court decided after establishing that the parcel of land approximately 15,994.5 acres in Taita Taveta was unlawfully auctioned in 2007 after the former MP defaulted on a loan.

Justices Roselyn Nambuye, Wanjiru Karanja, and Patrick Kiage said that the bank undervalued the parcel of land by selling it at KSh 55 million given that there were buildings, sisal, quarry, and a road network on the land, and rushed to dispose of it to recover KSh 20 million that the former legislator is said to have owed the bank.
