Yvonne Stern stands by Texas attorney husband Jeffrey who 'tried to kill her twice'

Publish date: 2024-09-15

'I wholeheartedly love him': Wife shot in the stomach and targeted THREE times stands by husband 'who plotted with his lover to have her killed'

By Inderdeep Bains for the Daily Mail
Updated: 18:20 EDT, 2 November 2011

He has been charged along with his mistress with hiring hitmen to kill her, but Yvonne Stern says she still loves her husband Jeffrey and vows to stand by his side.

Millionaire Texas attorney Mr Stern, 53, and former girlfriend Michelle Gaiser, 38, are charged over three attempts on the mother-of-two's life.

It is claimed that Gaiser plotted with Mr Stern, who is facing trial for two counts of soliciting capital murder, to have the 51-year-old socialite killed by a hitman.

Stand by your man: Yvonne Stern arrives at the police station with her husband Jeffrey as he hands himself over to officers investigating three murder attempts on her life

In total, Mrs Stern, survived three attempts on her life in six months - including being shot in the stomach by one of the assassins.

Mr Stern is currently free on $1million dollar bail and insists he had no involvement in the plot.

And Mrs Stern believes him. Convinced her husband was not involved in the plot against her, she says: 'I love my husband. I believe in him wholeheartedly, in his innocence. He had nothing to do with this. We are victims of a fatal attraction.'

'I love my husband. I believe in him wholeheartedly, in his innocence. We are victims of a fatal attraction.'

At the time of Gaiser's arrest, Mrs Stern filed for divorce, citing adultery. But just months later she changed her mind and insisted the couple were reconciled

The socialite is now standing by her husband as he faces trial later this year accused of trying to kill her and even accompanied him to the police station when he handed himself in.

The first murder attempt was in February last year, when a hitman blasted Mrs Stern's $800,000 Bellaire home in a drive-by shooting. The mother-of-two and her children were not at home and escaped uninjured.

Two months later gunman Nhut Nguyen rang the doorbell of her home and opened fire as she approached the door.

But Mrs Stern was able to jump for cover as he shot through the glass.

Love triangle: Michelle Gaiser, 38, and her lover Jeffrey Stern are accused of plotting to kill Stern's wife, Yvonne

According to his lawyer, Mr Stern then moved his family to an apartment for safety - but another assassin still tracked his wife down.

In May last year, the third gunman - which the prosecution claim is Damian Flores -  approached Mrs Stern as she sat in her car at their apartment's garage.

She begged and pleaded for her life and he appeared to have a change of heart, lowering the gun to her abdomen and shooting her in the stomach.

Bleeding, she only survived by playing dead then driving to the nearest petrol station for help.

Gaiser has already confessed to plotting to kill her lover's wife, and testified at Harris County Court in Houston today during the trial of Flores which started this week.

Wearing an orange jail jumpsuit, Gaiser said she hired Flores for $40,000 in May 2010 after two previous attempts to kill Stern failed.

She claimed that she had given him $15,000 in cash upfront and instructed him to go to an apartment where Mrs Stern had moved after the first two shootings.

'He was supposed to go over there and wait for Yvonne and do his job,' she said.

The tearful victim recalled yesterday how she had begged Flores for her life as she gave evidence in his trial.

'He jumped out of his car and came charging at me with a gun. He had a pair of mirrored aviators. He didn't run. He walked and came charging at me,' Mrs Stern said.

Shooting victim: Mrs Stern, 51, survived three attempts on her life - including being shot in the stomach by one of the assassins.

After giving evidence on Monday, Mrs Stern left the court holding hands with her husband and paused to tell reporters that she loved her husband and had forgiven him for 'his indiscretions.'

Gaiser is charged with capital murder solicitation, which is punishable by life in prison, and testified under a plea deal that carries a 25-year prison sentence.

'I made a mistake. Not a day goes by that I don't feel bad for what I've done. I feel bad for all the people I've hurt,' she told the jury.

However, Flores' attorney Sam Cammack has accused Gaiser of lying about his client being involved.

Referring to her plea deal, Mr Cammack said: 'She's saving her own butt. She was facing life in prison, so she has to come up with something.'

Gaiser is also expected to give evidence at the trial of Mr Stern, who faces a life sentence if convicted.

Gunman Nhut Nguyen, 38, has already been convicted of the previous attempts on Stern's life and has been sentenced to 45 years in prison.

Flores, who has been charged with attempted capital murder, could also be sentenced to life in prison if convicted.

Two other also face charges linked to the murder plot, including James Steven Lowery, 28, who is charged with solicitation of capital murder.

He claims to have helped broker the deal to kill Mrs Stern between Nguyen, Gaiser and Mr Stern.
