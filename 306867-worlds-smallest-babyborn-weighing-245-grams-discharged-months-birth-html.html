World's 'smallest baby' born weighing 245 grams discharged five months after birth

Publish date: 2024-04-12

It was joy all over after a girl believed to be the smallest ever surviving baby left neonatal intensive care after five months.

The baby, named Saybie by hospital staff, weighed only 8.6 ounces (245 grams) at the time she was born in San Diego, US.

Statistically, babies born with such development insufficiency have virtually no chances of survival but Saybie somehow beat the odds after being born at only 23 week.

According to Dr Edward Bell of the University of Iowa which maintains the tiny babies registry, Saybie is, as it stands, the tiniest ever recorded.

"Baby Saybie has the lowest birth weight among the infants in the Tiniest Babies Registry," Bell told NPR. "The registry contains only those infants submitted and medically confirmed. We cannot rule out even smaller infants who have not been reported to the registry." Bell said as quoted by NPR.

Further reports indicated the child’s mother, at only 23 weeks, developed complications and had to undergo emergency caesarean operation after it emerged the baby was not growing anymore.

The mother, at the time of the premature birth, refused to make her name public amid fears she might not survive.

At only 23 weeks , Saybie was born the size of a large apple and could easily fit in an adult male’s palms.

Predictably, doctors were very skeptical on the chances of her survival, with her parents being informed they had an hour with her before she passes away.

Five months later, however, the baby has been doing fairly well and amazed nurses planted a graduation cap on her as she left her crib dubbed “tiny but mighty”.

For those who are still wondering how a baby who had barely made it to the fetal stage in pregnancy managed to defy odds, it was a mixture of good luck coupled with good genes and, as nurses have put it, other inexplicable factors.
