Herms fashion heir facing 20 years in jail for 'grabbing pilot's crotch and threatening to punch h

Publish date: 2024-05-21

By Peter Allen for MailOnline
Updated: 06:49 EDT, 16 October 2008

Mathias Guerrand-Hermès, the heir to the fashion house that bears his name, is facing 20 years in prison after grabbing a pilot's crotch and trying to punch him

An heir to the Hermès fashion house is facing 20 years in prison after grabbing a pilot’s crotch and trying to punch him on route from Paris to New York.

Mathias Guerrand-Hermès, described by socialite friends as 'a real livewire who likes a laugh', became so unruly on the transatlantic flight that he had to be handcuffed to a seat in first class.

It took the captain and at least three other crew members to finally restrain Guerrand-Hermès, 36, whose greatest passion in life is polo.

‘He was like a man possessed,’ said a passenger on Air France Flight 008, which left the French capital's Charles de Gaulle airport on Tuesday evening.

‘Grabbing someone indiscriminately isn’t a good idea at the best of times - but especially not the man who’s trying to fly your plane.’

Guerrand-Hermès, who was on his way back to his apartment in New York to celebrate his 37th birthday on Sunday, began acting strangely around three hours into the flight.

According to charge papers filed in the U.S. he had drunk 'quite a bit of  alcohol', as well as the prescription drug, Propofan.

Initially Guerrand-Hermès perched on the armrest of a woman’s seat in his row in first-class, prompting her to start nudging her sleeping husband awake.

Victoria Beckham is often seen with an expensive Hermes Birkin bag.

It is alleged the husband told Guerrand-Hermès to go away, but he refused.

Flight attendants were called, and then the captain, who asked Guerrand-Hermès to calm down.

Instead he grabbed the pilot’s crotch, shouting ‘I am not going to behave myself’, before trying to throw a punch.  After a brief stuggle, Guerrand-Hermès was restrained by the flight crew who were able to restrain him with handcuffs and shackles, tying him to a seat.

On landing at John F. Kennedy airport in New York, Guerrand-Hermès was arrested by  FBI agents who charged him with one count of interfering with flight crew members, which carries a maximum penalty of 20 years in prison.

His father is Patrick Guerrand-Hermès, president of the  Federation of International Polo.

Known as a super-rich "trust fund son" by friends, Guerrand-Hermès married in Paris in 1999, with a five-day reception in Marrakech, Morocco, to which 450  guests were flown by private jet.

He is believed to be currently working for American investment group, Blue Growth Capital.  Guerrand-Hermès was charged on Wednesday afternoon at the Federal District Court in Brooklyn, New York.

Magistrate Judge Lois Bloom allowed Guerrand-Hermès to be released on bail of  £29,000.

A Hermès spokesman said Guerrand-Hermès currently had no direct role in the  company, and would not comment further.
