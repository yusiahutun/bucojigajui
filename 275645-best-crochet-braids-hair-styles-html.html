Over the last few years, 90s hairstyles have made a major comeback. And women of all ages are loving and embracing these iconic hairstyles. One of those hairstyle trends that has made a big come back and doesn't look like its fading anytime soon is crochet braids. What makes crochet braids a must have hairstyle for any lady is that it’s so realistic. Which means it has a natural look that will make the braids appear as if it’s your real hair? Unlike other hairstyles that take a whole day to prepare, crochet braids styles have a quicker application process and put minimum stress on your hair. The hairstyle also requires little maintenance yet can last up to 8 weeks.

Before you can dive into the latest crochet braid hairstyles, first you have to understand what crochet braids are. Don't confuse them with box braids or twists because crochet braids are hair extensions that are crocheted into your natural hair using a latch hook. Your natural hair will have to be braided into cornrows onto which the extensions will then be added.

What differentiates crochet braids from weaves is that crochet hair extension is looped under the cornrows using a hook while a weave you have to sew it using a needle and thread. 6. Crochet box braids hairstyles

When your natural hair needs a long break from all the pulling and heat, then crochet box braids are the best way to go. Whether short or long, box braids look amazing on both older and younger ladies. Since your natural hair will be in cornrows, the crochet box braids act as a protective style. The best part about opting for crochet box braids is that you don’t have to spend a lot of time in the salon, as this hairstyle can take less than 3 hours to make yet with proper maintenance can last even for two months. You can choose between waist length, long, medium and even micro braids.

7. Voluminous crochet micro braids

Which girl or lady hasn’t tried those thin and delicate micro box braids! The main reason why we go for the thin braids is that we want to forget about going to the salon for the next few months. The bad news is the standard micro braids are tightly woven on the scalp which tends to damage your natural hair. On the other hand instead of clearing out your whole day to get micro braids or worrying about hair damage you can now get these braids in a matter of few hours through crocheting. Crochet micro braids look as natural as the standard plaited ones and can even create the illusion of natural hair if you style the baby hair on the front and side sections.
