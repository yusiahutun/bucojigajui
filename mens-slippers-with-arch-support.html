What to Look for in Slippers With Arch Support, According to a Podiatrist

Aside from a footbed that supports the shape of your foot, Miller adds that “improved alignment and lessened foot pressure can be achieved with a firm sole that is slightly elevated at the heel.”

She also recommends paying attention to materials and looking for slippers that “use breathable, natural textiles,” which will help to keep feet dry and comfortable.

Maurice Verhagen, owner and certified podiatrist at Netherlands-based Penninx Schoenen, adds that it’s important to know whether your arch type is low, medium, or high, this way you can select a slipper that provides the perfect amount of support. He also recommends taking into account heel height when shopping.

“A minimum heel height of 15 to 20mm is ideal to support the arch and improve pressure distribution while walking or standing,” Verhagen says.

Why Wear Slippers With Arch Support?

“Choosing the right slipper is paramount for maintaining good foot health and preventing foot-related issues,” Verhagen tells StyleCaster.

Having slippers with arch support also creates a good foundation, which works to distribute weight evenly and helps with balance. Not having enough arch support may also contribute to back problems.

What to Avoid in Slippers if You Need Arch Support

“Be careful of memory foam!” warns Joshua Lisonbee, DPM, a podiatrist who practices in the Seattle area. “There’s nothing inherently wrong with memory foam, but it’s not providing much support either. A lot of brands like to advertise this feature as a cure-all for sore feet, but most memory foam compresses down quickly under your body weight and stops being effective after just a few minutes. If all they’ve done is put a layer of memory foam inside an otherwise flat shoe, it’s going to feel good while
you’re trying it on in the store, but disastrous once you bring it home and start using it more regularly.”

Miller also tells us that it might be better to wait until later in the day to test out any slippers.

“To make sure slippers fit properly, my general recommendation is to try them on toward the end of the day when your feet are usually at their widest,” she says.
