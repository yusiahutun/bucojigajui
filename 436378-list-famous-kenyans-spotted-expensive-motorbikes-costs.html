William Kabogo, Other Famous Kenyans Spotted with Expensive Motorbikes and Their Costs

Publish date: 2024-07-14

  • Former Kiambu governor William Kabogo lives life on the fast lane and owns a luxurious sport bike worth KSh 2 million
  • B Club billionaire entrepreneur Barry Ndengenye's multi-million-shilling convertibles include a Range Rover, Rolls Royce, Lamborghini, Ferrari and a futuristic Tron bike

When it comes to spending money on luxurious rides, Kenyan politicians and celebrities never disappoint.

Some wealthy, famous Kenyans have never shied away from displaying their opulence on social media.

According to the United Nations Environmental Programme (UNEP), the number of motorcycles in Kenya is expected to hit five million by 2030.

The prices of motorbikes range from KSh 70,000 to over KSh 1 million, depending on the model and features.

TUKO.co.ke list some famous Kenyans who have shown off their superbikes that cost an arm and a leg.

1. William Kabogo

Former Kiambu governor William Kabogo lives life on the fast lane as shown during his past interview with TUKO.co.ke.

After losing his bid to retain his seat in the 2017 General Election, the politician hung out with his expensive machines.

Kabogo is a biker who enjoys riding in Kiambu's tea plantations.

In one instance, he treated Kiambu residents to a spectacle after visiting a local joint with Ducati sports bike whose worth is estimated at KSh 2 million as per The Standard.

In May 2019, he also displayed another R1 bike on his social media pages.

According to Cycleworld, the manufacturer's suggested retail price of R1 is $17,399 (KSh 1.95 million), whereas the up-spec YZF-R1M comes in at $26,099 (KSh 2.9 million).

Besides the bikes, Kabogo has other expensive luxury sports cars worth millions of shillings.

He acquired his Jaguar XKR at KSh 15 million, while his armoured military car has a market value of over KSh 45 million. He also has a Hummer double cap that he used to campaign in the 2017 polls.

2. Shaffie Weru

In April 2021, media personality Shaffie Weru revealed he earned a monthly salary of KSh 682,000 after he was dismissed from HomeBoyz Radio for airing what was regarded as insensitive content on live radio.

With a huge salary, Shaffie has always lived a flashy lifestyle and has a thing for powerful machines.

In January 2017, he showed off his brand new Harley Davidson bike, which reportedly cost him over KSh 1 million after-tax.

Some of the bike's features are a V-twin engine, optional Anti-Lock Brakes (ABS), and an air cooling system.

In June, he shared photos and videos of his daughter Milan in her brand new Jeep Wrangler worth KSh 3 million.

It was not yet clear if Shaffie bought the car for Milan single-handedly or got some little help from her mum Debbie.

3. Barry Ndengenye

The B Club billionaire entrepreneur owns several multi-million-shilling convertibles.

His collection includes a Range Rover, Rolls Royce, Lamborghini, Ferrari and a futuristic Tron bike.

The bike manufactured by Parker Brothers Choppers in the US costs between KSh 5.5 million to KSh 10 million.

Most expensive cars

TUKO.co.ke previously reported Kenyan roads are filled with super expensive and sleek rides worth millions of shillings.

The Bugatti Veyron Grand Sport Vitesse is one of the most expensive cars being driven on Kenyan roads.

President Uhuru Kenyatta has been regularly spotted being driven in the Mercedes-Benz Pullman S600 worth over KSh 160 million.
