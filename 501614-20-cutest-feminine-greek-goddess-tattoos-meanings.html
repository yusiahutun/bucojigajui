20 cutest feminine Greek goddess tattoos with their meanings

Publish date: 2024-06-09

Female tattoos have become increasingly popular over time. More women are adorning their bodies with intricate designs with meaningful symbols. One such popular design is the female Greek goddess tattoos, which have gained fame recently. But what are the meanings behind these tattoos?

Greek goddesses' body art has become a symbol of strength and feminity for most women in the modern world. These designs have unique legends that have resonated well with women seeking inspiration. Getting feminine Greek goddess tattoos is ideal if you want unique yet meaningful body art.

Feminine Greek goddess tattoos

Different known Greek goddesses exist, each unique and equipped with various powers. Examples include the Olympian goddesses of Hera and Athena to the primordial ones like Gaia and Nyx. But with such an extensive collection, which are the most meaningful Greek goddess tattoo for ladies?

1. Athena goddess tattoo

Who is the goddess of female empowerment? Athena is the deity of wisdom, courage, and warfare that embodies empowerment. She symbolises strength, intelligence and strategy. Her helmet and shield represent strength, while the owl signifies wisdom and judgement.

2. Aphrodite

What does the Aphrodite tattoo mean? The design symbolises femininity, sensuality, and romantic love. Aphrodite was the goddess of love, beauty, procreation, desire and pleasure. She appears accompanied by the winged godling Eros.

3. Hera

Hera is the goddess of marriage, family, and childbirth, symbolising loyalty, commitment, and motherhood. She was the queen of Olympus and was very powerful. Ancient women worshipped Hera for good health and protection during childbirth. Her symbols include pomegranate fruit, a sceptre and her crown.

4. Demeter

The Demeter tattoo symbolises growth, abundance, and fertility. According to Greek mythology, Demeter was the deity of agriculture and harvest daughter of Kronos and Rhea. One ideal design is getting the body art of Demeter holding the Cornucopia, the horn of plenty.

5. Artemis

What Greek goddess represents femininity? Artemis is the deity that embodies femininity. She is the goddess of the hunt, chastity, wilderness, and childbirth. She was the daughter of Zeus and Leto and the twin sister of Apollo. In most of her depictions, Artemis holds a bow, arrow and a short tunic to aid her as she navigates the woods. Her body art symbolises independence, strength, and protection.

6. Persephone

Persephone is the daughter of Demeter and Zeus and the wife of Hades. She is the queen of the underworld and goddess of spring. A Demeter body art symbolises transformation, rebirth, and renewal. Because of her dual deity, you can get a Persephone tattoo holding vegetation or presiding over the dead.

7. Hestia

Hestia is the daughter of Kronos and Rhea and the goddess of the hearth, hospitality and home. She maintained the hearth fire of Mount Olympus and the homes of the Greeks. Hestia symbolises warmth, hospitality, and domesticity.

8. Gaia

Gaia is among the Greek primordial deities and is the ancestral mother of all life. She is the goddess of the earth symbolising nature, growth, and abundance. Gaia could create life and had three children. They include the heavens (Uranus), the seas (Pontus) and the mountains.

9. Nemesis

Nemesis is among the most popular body art designs favoured by women looking to settle a score. Nemesis was a goddess of revenge and balance, and her presence symbolised justice, retribution, and harmony.

10. Nike

Nike, the winged goddess of victory, is the daughter of Pallas (son of the Titan Crius and Eurybia) and Styx. She symbolises triumph, success, and accomplishment. Nike's traits include the ability to fly, as the power of speed and embodies victory.

11. Eris

If you feel bad*ss or want a savage tattoo, then the Eris goddess design is perfect. Eris is the deity of chaos and discord, symbolising upheaval, change, and transformation. She can cause confusion and hatred and is known for her part in starting the Trojan War.

12. Iris

According to Greek mythology, Iris was the daughter of Thaumas and the ocean nymph Electra. She is the goddess of the rainbow symbolising beauty, hope, and promise. She is the personification of the rainbow and the messenger to the gods. Iris symbols include the rainbow, the caduceus and the pitcher.

13. Selene or Mene

Selene, the daughter of Titans Hyperion and Theia, is the personification of the moon. She is the titan goddess of the moon and symbolises intuition, mysticism, and magic. According to legend, she appears as a woman riding a chariot drawn by winged horses.

14. D*ke or Dice

The D*ke goddess drawing is perfect if you want a tattoo representing fairness, truth, and righteousness. D*ke is the daughter of Zeus and Themis and the Greek divinity of justice, morals and order. The goddess appears as a young woman carrying a balance scale.

15. Hebe

Hebe is the daughter of Zeus and Hera. She is the deity of youth and symbolises vitality, energy, and innocence. Hebe is often depicted as an eagle and is the cupbearer for the gods and goddesses of Mount Olympus.

16. Rhea

Rhea is the mother goddess and the daughter of the earth divinity Gaia and the sky god Uranus. She is the deity of motherhood and fertility, symbolising nurturing, care, and fertility. Rhea's symbols include the chariot, tambourine, crown, and Cornucopia.

17. Amphitrite

If you are looking for a spiritual goddess tattoo symbolising power and mystery, then the Amphitrite divinity drawing is ideal. Amphitrite is the deity of the sea and represents might, mystery, and depth.

18. Mnemosyne

Mnemosyne, the goddess of memory, has power and control over all memories. She represents remembrance, history, and wisdom and also can transverse time. Mnemosyne often appears holding a lamp of knowledge.

19. Theia

20. Tyche

The Tyche deity design is perfect if you are looking for small tattoos of Greek mythology symbolising luck. Tyche is the goddess of luck and fortune, representing good fortune, prosperity, and abundance.
