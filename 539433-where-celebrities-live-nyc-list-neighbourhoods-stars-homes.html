1. Upper East Side of Manhattan

The Upper East Side is a neighbourhood in the borough of Manhattan in New York City. It is renowned for its affluent residential areas, high-end shopping, prestigious museums, and cultural institutions. Many celebrities have chosen to call this their home over the years. They include.

Mariah Carey

You will find Mariah Carey on the Upper East Side of the borough. Mariah Carey is a songwriter and musician born and raised in Huntington, New York. She began honing her singing and songwriting talents at a tender age, lending her voice for backing vocals as early as 12.

She is among the celebrities with homes in Tribeca, a luxurious side of Manhattan. The celebrity possesses a four-bedroom, four-and-a-half-bathroom triplex penthouse in the Franklin Tower at 90 Franklin Street in Tribeca. Acquired in the mid-2000s for a sum exceeding $9 million, she boasts a 38-foot-long bathroom and a media room.

Robert DeNiro

Robert De Niro is a big name in movies from New York. He was born in the Bronx in 1943. He lives in one of the fanciest buildings in the city. His apartment is in Manhattan, right by Central Park West near the Museum of Natural History. Other famous people like Denzel Washington and Sting also live there.

Taylor Swift

Taylor Swift has purchased properties in Tribeca, mainly at 155 Franklin Street in the Sugar Loaf Building. The singer owns a two-story penthouse and part of the second floor. Taylor Swift bought the two top-floor units together in 2014 for $19.95 million, making her large home spacious, covering 8,000 square feet altogether.

Martin Scorsese

Martin Scorsese is an American filmmaker and director well known for his harsh, often violent depictions of American culture. Born in Queens in 1942 and raised in Little Italy, Martin is among many celebrities owning celebrity homes in NYC. Scorsese paid $12.5 million for a 7,000-square-foot townhouse in the Upper East Side of NYC and has lived there since then.

Drew Barrymore

Drew Barrymore is an American actress, producer, director, author, and entrepreneur. Drew was born in California, but she owns a duplex at 830 Park Avenue in New York City.

2. Upper West Side of Manhattan

This is one of the New York neighborhoods that attract celebrities. It offers an exceptional living experience: stunning prewar architecture, bordered by Central Park on one side and Riverside Park on the other. It stands as one of Manhattan's most sought-after residential districts and serves as a haven for celebrities. Below are some of the celebrities that live here.

Amy Schumer

Amy Schumer is a fantastic blonde comedian, writer, and actor. She is best known for her raunchy stand-up routines. She has acted in Trainwreck, I Feel Pretty, and Growing. The comedian is among celebrities who live in New York City. She purchased a 4,500-square-foot penthouse that cost her $12.15 million.

3. Greenwich Village in Manhattan

Greenwich exudes history, art, and diverse architecture, attracting residents for its central location near Soho, Chelsea, Union Square, and Tribeca. Despite its evolution from an artist haven, it remains a celebrity hotspot cherished for its beauty and cultural accessibility.

4. North of Houston

North of Houston is an absolute paradise. Three heritage zones preserve the area's history, limiting buildings to six stories. Its artistic ambience attracts residents, including celebrities like Cynthia Nixon, Gigi Hadid, and Jonah Hill.

5. South of Houston

SoHo, or South of Houston, offers top-tier shopping, dining, and historic architecture in NYC. This is home to many celebrities like Bella Hadid, who purchased a SoHo penthouse at 63 Greene Street in late 2019. Past Soho residents include Alicia Keys, Swizz Beatz, Daniel Craig, Jon Bon Jovi, John Mayer, and Justin Timberlake.

6. Brooklyn Borough

Situated to the west of Manhattan, accessible via the renowned Brooklyn Bridge, Brooklyn is often stereotyped as a haven for affluent families and trendy hipsters who prefer to eschew mainstream Manhattan. While there is some truth to this perception, Brooklyn also boasts significant cultural influence and shares a more laid-back atmosphere than its neighbouring borough.

7. The Bronx Borough

The Bronx, known for being the birthplace of the New York Yankees and the origin of hip-hop, is also renowned for its expansive parklands, including the city's largest park. Positioned geographically to the north of Manhattan, spanning over the Harlem River, this borough offers a wealth of distinctive attractions that contribute to its unique character.

These include Pelham Bay Park, the Bronx Zoo, Yankee Stadium, and abundant cultural experiences that enrich the lives of its residents. This borough hosts many celebrities such as Tracy Morgan, Cardi B, Dominic Chianese, Al Pacino, American rapper Fat Joe, and the musician Jennifer Lopez.

8. Staten Island Borough

Staten Island, often referred to as the "forgotten borough," is the least densely populated among New York City's five boroughs. Despite its relatively modest size, Staten Island has been the birthplace of numerous accomplished individuals who have left a notable imprint on the world.

Despite its small size, the island hosts numerous musicians and actors such as the Wu-Tang Clan, Christina Aguilera, business mogul Cornelius Vanderbilt, American actor Eddie Kaye Thomas, and Marcus Browne, among others.

9. Queens Borough

Queens Borough, one of the most diverse areas in New York City, has become a popular residential choice for celebrities seeking a balance between privacy and accessibility. Renowned for its vibrant neighbourhoods, cultural richness, and proximity to Manhattan, Queens offers an attractive blend of urban amenities and suburban tranquillity.

Celebrities often gravitate towards Queens for its spacious homes, leafy streets, and relative seclusion compared to the bustling heart of Manhattan. Some of the celebrities you might meet on these streets include the famous radio shock jock Howard Stern, actress Lori Loughlin, rapper 50 Cent, and comedian Ray Romano.
