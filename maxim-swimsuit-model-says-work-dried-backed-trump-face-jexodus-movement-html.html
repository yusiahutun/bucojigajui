Maxim swimsuit model says work dried up after she backed Trump and became face of 'Jexodus' movement

Publish date: 2024-06-29

The Maxim swimsuit model who started a movement to turn Democrat Jews Republican, has said she 'couldn't work' after admitting she was involved in President Trump's campaign.

Elizabeth Pipko, 24, revealed in January that she had started The Exodus Movement but the publicity had negative consequences for her career in front of the camera.

'After I "came out" [in support of President Trump] I noticed most of my modeling contacts quickly turn their backs on me,' she told Fox News. 'It's super similar to Hollywood in that sense, so I was prepared for it.'

Pipko appears in Los Angeles-based fashion and lifestyle magazine, QP.

She said: 'Honestly, after all the Trump campaign stuff came out I knew no one wanted me to model for them. I got so so excited when you guys said you'd have me in QP'

The publication accepts submissions from photographers and models who upload photo shoots via a networking website for creatives. But the photographer says in this case the feature about Pipko was assigned by the publication.

The article titled 'Don't Call It A Comeback' features Pipko posing in images shot by Nayo Martinez.

'I'm really excited that someone was able to put political views aside and see me as someone who is much more than just what the media wants people to think a conservative woman can be,' she told Fox News about the QP spread. 

Pipko said the opportunity was a chance to 'show people that I can still do it and that women can do anything'.

She told QP: 'I could not really work anymore because I let people know that I worked on the president's campaign which is incredibly unpopular in modeling'. Pipko appears in her shoot which was a chance to 'show people that I can still do it and that women can do anything'

Pipko said in a social media post about her new magazine shoot: 'In the world I've entered into, a world that chews people up and spits them out every single day, it is up to us to make sure that we are always at the table, but never on the menu'

'Honestly, after all the Trump campaign stuff came out I knew no one wanted me to model for them,' she admitted to QP. 'I got so so excited when you guys said you'd have me in QP.' 

'It's something I enjoy and would like to do much more of whenever possible. Not full time anymore though of course since my life has changed so much,' she added.

Pipko wrote on Instagram that she won't let anyone take the modeling memories away from her

Pipko started working as a model aged 17, worked on Trump's 2016 campaign when she was 20 and has since started her own SuperPac which focuses on Jewish Americans.

Pipko - who married Darren Centinello at Mar-A-Lago last December, describes Trump as 'incredibly big on loyalty' and 'someone who supports anyone who has ever supported him in any way'.  

Asked why she had stopped modeling, Pipko admitted it was because her affiliation was 'unpopular' in the world of modeling.

'I stopped because of a number of reasons. First of all I wanted to do something bigger and focus on something much more important like our country,' she told QP. 'Second, I could not really work anymore because I let people know that I worked on the president's campaign which is incredibly unpopular in the modeling world.'

Pipko added that 'politics can be a lot' but said she's doing 'something important to me which is what matters'.

The backlash hasn't put Pipko off politics though. She laughed that she would get involved in another campaign, 'possibly... if Kanye ran'.

In a social media post promoting her latest modeling work, Pipko said she plans to take her modeling experience with her wherever she goes and that she won't let anyone take the memories away from her.

'I never plan on apologizing for my modeling career, more importantly I don't plan on letting anyone take those memories away from me,' she posted alongside a snap of the shoot. 'I was proud to be a part of a world so artistic and extravagant, and a world that prepared me in many ways for the challenges that I face today.'

Pipko posted about the confidence modeling gave her after dealing with anxiety and depression in her teens.
