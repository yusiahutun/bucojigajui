'Call everyone Dave day' set up in memory of Only Fools' Trigger actor Roger Lloyd-Pack

Publish date: 2024-05-24

The event has been organised by a Facebook page set up to honour the late Lloyd-Pack, who died two weeks ago aged 69.

On February 8, thousands of fans of the show will become cult hero Trigger for one day and one day only.

The date, which would have been Lloyd-Pack's 70th birthday, will see fans calling everyone else Dave during their daily tasks.

More than 13,000 people like the page and #DaveDay has been trending on social networking sites.

Lloyd-Pack's dimwitted character struck fame by calling Rodney Trotter, Dave, which became a regular joke in the long-running sitcom.

Trigger's best dave moments include the time when he asked Rodney what he and partner Cassandra were going to call their unborn child.

Rodney said he would call the baby Rodney is it was a boy after him.

To which Trigger announced: "He's naming it Rodney after Dave."

One Facebook user suggested anyone who is named "Dave" should be called "Rodney" for the special occasion.

Lloyd-Pack lost his battle with pancreatic cancer on January 15.

The star died in his home in Kentish Town with his family surrounding him.

He was also well-known for his part in Vicar of Dibley as farmer Owen Newitt, among other things.

He featured in The Bill, Inspector Morse and in one of the Harry Potter films.

Dave Day is fundraising on JustGiving to raise money for Pancreatic Cancer UK.

It is urging Facebook followers to donate to the good cause.
