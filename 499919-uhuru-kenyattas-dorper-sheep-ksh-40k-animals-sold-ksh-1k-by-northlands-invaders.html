Uhuru Kenyatta's Dorper Sheep: KSh 40k Animals Sold for KSh 1k by Northlands Invaders

Publish date: 2024-02-18
  • Uhuru Kenyatta's farm in Ruiru, Kiambu county, was invaded by goons who destroyed and stole property
  • The group cut down trees, destroyed the irrigation system and carted away Dorper sheep and goats
  • The invaders sold the Dorper sheep along the Eastern Bypass at a throwaway price, unaware of their worth

On Monday, March 27, suspected goons invaded a farm in Ruiru, Kiambu county owned by former president Uhuru Kenyatta's family.

Uhuru's Dorper sheep sold at throwaway price

The group gained access to the farm from the Kamaki’s side after destroying a barbed wire fence.

The suspected goons cut down trees using power saws, set the farm on fire and were seen carrying sheep and goats from the vast land.

Footage captured outside the farm showed a huge number of motorists stopping their vehicles to purchase the proceeds of the crime.

Citizen TV reports that the goons were selling the Dorper sheep for as low as KSh 1,000 unaware of the animals’ true value.

According to Farm Biz Africa, the price of the breed varies depending on the season. Averagely, a fully grown pure Dorper sheep costs KSh 45,000 for a ram, KSh. 25,000 for the ewe and KSh 6,000 for a lamb.

A farmer rearing the sheep told the publication that Dorper sheep have a good mothering ability and exhibit a long breeding season.

They also have good adaptability and are non-selective grazers- making maximum use of available pastures.

A mature Doepr ram can weigh 80kg and 114 kg with the eves weighing 57kg to 80 kg.

The Northlands City

Brookside Dairy and Peponi School are located on the property and the land is part of the Kenyatta family’s plan to construct Northlands City.
