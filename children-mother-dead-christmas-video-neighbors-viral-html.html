Four children whose mother DIED just weeks before Christmas are gifted more than $150,000 by strange

Publish date: 2024-07-19

The story of four siblings who lost their mother just weeks before Christmas inspired thousands to donate money so her kids could have a better future.

Widespread attention was brought to the family's plight after a heart-rendering Ring doorbell video of eldest child Micah, who lost his mom, Sheneice Simms, just weeks before the holidays, wishing his neighbors a 'Merry Christmas' went viral.

In the clip, captured about 6:30 p.m. on Christmas Day, Micah is seen ringing the bell at his neighbors' front door, only to have an automated answering machine inform him that no one was home.

Prompted to leave a message, he adorably responds: 'Merry Christmas! That's all I gotta say. I wanted to make myself happier by coming out to say Merry Christmas. My mom died a couple weeks back. That's all I had to say: Merry Christmas. I like the decorations on your door.'

A boy named Micah went to his neighbors' front door to wish them Merry Christmas in an attempt to cheer himself up in the aftermath of his mother's death

On that same evening, Micah's neighbor Sharon Gonzalez posted the clip to her Facebook

It wasn't long before Sharon's post went viral. After she got in touch with Micah's aunt Sherell Ferrer, she linked to a GoFundMe to raise money to support the kids going forward

That same night, the neighbor, Sharon Gonzalez, posted the clip to her personal Facebook page with a brief note.

'This young boy knocked on my door today while we were away,' she wrote alongside the video.

'I don't know him but his message broke my heart.

'First I want to say Merry Christmas to you too, and I'm sorry for your mother's loss I know what it's like not having your loved ones with you during the holidays.'

'I know you said you're doing this to feel happy but you made me happy as well,' her note concluded.

Soon, the post started to go viral, and many commented that they were touched by the video - and that they'd be more than happy to help out the boy's family.

Just a couple days later, Sharon updated the post, explaining that she'd located Micah's aunt Sherell Ferrer. With that, Sharon linked to a GoFundMe to raise money for Sheneice's kids in the aftermath of their loss, as well as to their Amazon Wish List.

The GoFundMe summary further explained that the mother, Sheneice Simms, had 'unexpectedly passed away' at age 33 on November 29, 'leaving behind four young children who now face a future without their beloved mother.' 

The cause of her death is unknown.

Micah's mom Sheneice Simms passed away at age 33 on November 29, 2023, leaving behind four kids along with other family members

The cause of Sheneice's death was not publicized by her surviving family

Contributors to the family's GoFundMe shared their condolences to eldest son Micah and his siblings following the recent loss of their mom

'Sheneice was the heart and soul of her family, a devoted mother who worked tirelessly to provide for her children. Her sudden departure has left a void that can never be filled, and the grief the kids are experiencing is immeasurable,' it continued.

In the less than one week since its creation, the GoFundMe has so far raked in almost $150,000 from nearly 5,000 donors - with several people making individual donations of $1,000.

Comments left with the donations expressed heartfelt well wishes to Sheneice's children, with many emphasizing they were moved by the video of Micah wishing the Gonzalez family a Merry Christmas.

'Sending you all my condolences, love and prayers. May god be with your family comforting you guys and giving you peace during this difficult time,' one wrote.

'The world needs more people like Micah. You're all going to be okay,' a second offered.

'I saw the video on IG and was immediately brought to tears. This young man seems to have such a kind, gentle heart and I pray that God blesses him and his siblings. Although I have not lost a parent, I know what it's like to lose loved ones around the holidays and the lasting emptiness it causes. I hope these children feel loved and cared for and can find peace in knowing their mom did her best raising them. God bless!' a third chimed in.

A fourth added: 'To the kids of this family, you are loved by many. Your mom's spirit is with you all. May God continue to shower you with his love, and grace!'
