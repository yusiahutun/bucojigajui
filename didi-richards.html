DiDi Richards- Professional Career

DiDi led Cypress Ranch High School in scoring as a senior, averaging 18.5 points, 8.2 rebounds, 2.3 assists, and 2.9 steals per game. She was a five-star recruit who also made the 2017 McDonald’s All-American team and the Jordan Brand Classic. DiDi attended Baylor for the duration of her college career.

As a freshman, she appeared in 33 games and averaged 5.1 points, 4.3 rebounds, and 1.6 assists per game. At the end of the season, DiDi led the Lady Bears in thefts (six), blocks (three), and assists (one). She finished third in blocks and rebounds among Big 12 newcomers, making a mark on the whole conference. DiDi had a breakout season in her sophomore year.

She started all 38 of her games and contributed to the Lady Bears’ championship win over Notre Dame. During the season, she was named Defensive Player of the Year and was named to the Big 12 All-Defensive Team. DiDi’s reputation as one of the nation’s best on-ball defenders was cemented when she won the Naismith and WBCA National Defensive Player of the Year Awards.

Even though her season was cut short by the COVID-19, her 171 assists rank sixth all-time among Baylor juniors. She played in and started all 30 games at Baylor, averaging a team-high 29.9 minutes per game.

WNBA

DiDis’ final season at Baylor before starting her WNBA career was one of tenacity. During preseason practice, DiDi collided with a teammate, immobilizing him for a moment. Despite being told to consider life after basketball, she pushed herself and returned to the court 38 days later.

She led the Lady Bears to the Elite Eight this season, averaging 6.3 points, 6.3 assists, and little over one steal per game. In the 2021 WNBA Draft, the New York Liberty selected “Welcome to NY” with the 17th overall pick. participated in 31 games as a rookie and finished first among rookies in three-point field goal percentage, games played, assists, and minutes played. She was chosen to the Associated Press All-Rookie Team in appreciation of her great year.
