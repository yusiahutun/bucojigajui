Cross-dressing muggers, armed with pepper spray, attack park-goers 'in broad daylight'

By Daily Mail Reporter
Updated: 07:02 EST, 28 November 2011

What was supposed to be a nice daytime stroll went haywire for two people when they were attacked by transvestites armed with pepper spary.

Dressed in drag, Brandon Arnold, 24, and roommate Michael Burns, 21, mugged a teenage girl in Lincoln Park Zoo.

This occurred moments after they assaulted another pedestrian and tried to pilfer his wallet, prosecutors said.

Cross-dressing muggers: Michael Burns, 21, and Brandon Arnold, 24, were charged with robbery and attempted armed robbery after attacking two people

The pair arrived at Cook County Central Bond Court in Chicago, donning styled hair and gray leggings, to be charged with robbery and attempted armed robbery.

According to the courts, dressed in womens clothes, they had attacked a man in the 2600 block of North Cannon Drive.

Grabbing him from behind, they wrestled for his wallet until the attacked was able to break free with his wallet intact, the victim alleged.

‘When I turned around, one was holding the can of mace, but it must have jammed or it didn't work,’ he told the Chicago Tribune.

He ran and was pursued on foot by the unlikely robbing duo until they lost him.

Moments later, at 2:24 p.m, they approached a 16-year-old girl at under the pretence of asking for directions.

They raised a can of pepper spray and said: ‘Give me your camera or I'll mace you.’

Mr Arnold also demanded the girls iPhone, prosecutors alleged.

She gave both items up and the twosome sprinted down Fullerton Avenue.

She was following them when a squad car, responding to the male victims call, arrived. 

Mr Burns and Mr Arnold dropped the phone, camera and pepper spray in some bushes, court records said.

‘It wasn't funny at all,’ the male victim said Saturday. ‘These guys attacked two people, minutes apart, in broad daylight in the park.'
